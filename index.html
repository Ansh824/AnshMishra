About

I design and validate dynamic systems operating in acoustically and mechanically complex environments. My work integrates theoretical modeling, experimental validation, and advanced signal processing to study and control high-frequency mechanical systems.

My experience spans aerospace acoustics, ultrasonic communication, combustion instability, underwater acoustic sensing, and vibration-driven smart structures. I specialize in building experimental rigs, conducting modal analysis using Scanning Laser Doppler Vibrometry (SLDV), and developing automation tools that dramatically reduce analysis time while improving accuracy.

I am currently a Graduate Research Assistant at Georgia Tech working on ultrasonic communication modules for bioinspired robotic systems in collaboration with ONR and Sandia National Laboratories.

Selected Work

Ultrasonic Communication for Piezoelectric Robotic Fish
Graduate Research Assistant — Smart Structures & Dynamical Systems Lab

Developing an ultrasonic communication module enabling remote control of a multi-DOF bioinspired robotic fish. Conducting vibration analysis and advanced signal processing on piezoelectric-actuated tail structures using Scanning Laser Doppler Vibrometry (SLDV) to characterize modal response, thrust generation, and dynamic behavior.

Flight Acoustics & Automation — Gulfstream Aerospace
Innovation Engineering & Flight Intern

Conducted in-flight cabin noise surveys and analyzed flyover noise certification data using MATLAB. Developed automation scripts reducing acoustic data processing time from 2 hours to 30 seconds (99% efficiency improvement). Performed engine tonal analysis using ArtemiS Suite and correlated tonal content with cabin acoustic levels.

High-Voltage Power Distribution Thermal-Vibration Testing — Sensata Technologies
Mechanical Engineering Intern

Designed experimental setups to correlate far-field sensor data with internal component temperatures in high-voltage power distribution units (HVDU). Conducted vibration and mechanical shock testing to evaluate potting and thermal interface materials. Developed analytical models in MATLAB and Ansys and validated results through physical testing.

Underwater Acoustic Identification Tags for AUV Navigation
Naval Underwater Acoustics Lab

Designed and optimized passive acoustic identification tags for autonomous underwater vehicle docking systems. Used MATLAB and LabVIEW for backscatter signal analysis, improving tag uniqueness and localization accuracy. Conducted directionality and sensitivity testing for transducers and hydrophones.
